Sleep

I get an average of 5 hours of sleep per day. Five hours.

It wasn't always like this. There were times when I slept around 8 hours a day. That's the recommended number of hours of sleep one must get right? I heard that different people tend to sleep different amounts of time, but I'm pretty sure that 5 hours is not in the set of all recommended durations of sleep for a human being. I know this because the consequences of less sleep are evident and visible. I shall not get into the details of what they are, because I would like to live in denial for some more time. Why? Because I like losing sleep.

Do you like losing sleep? If you think no, think again. I don't like losing sleep either, but that's because I want to reap the benefits of having a good sleep schedule. Who wouldn't want better focus, better immunity, better fitness, better mental health, better composure, lesser headaches, and oh, lesser hairfall? I do, but I also want the time that I get to use in exchange for my sleep time. Why?

Because I am in control of that time.

After spending the entire day fulfilling responsibilities, granting requests, doing tasks that you are supposed to do, wouldn't it be amazing to have some time to yourself? Time that you can spend any which way you want? After an exhausting day, I get very tempted to sacrifice sleep and watch an engaging thriller movie, or a sitcom to put my mind at ease. I tend to forget everything else when I'm watching these shows, and that's exactly what makes this experience so alluring. A sleepless night spent watching movies and tv shows gives me control over myself and gives me a break from my hectic life. I think I can be more precise.

Such a sleepless night gives me an illusion of control and a means to flee from reality.

The human mind is a pretty good negotiator. It ties up a beautiful story, promises a great time and manipulates your hormones so elegantly that you end up signing the sleepless night contract. But remember, never evaluate a contract by its promises, do it with its results. The next morning, you wake up with a feeling of dread. Heck, sometimes you don't even wake up and later regret not waking up early. You skip meals. You lose focus. You can't study properly. You don't ask doubts in class because you believe missed lots of what has been covered already, and that's the reason you don't understand anything. You think "Classes are getting difficult, I really need to study by myself to keep up". You think the same thing in every class and build up stress. You see an assignment and nothing makes sense to you. You get back home and lie on the bed and think "Damn, today was stressful". That's the cue for your inner negotiator to start talking.

"You seem stressed. Want to watch a movie and escape reality?"

And the cycle begins all over again, this time with even more stress. It makes your life more stressful, even miserable, but the cycle won't stop. You might ask "If it's such a bad thing, why do we keep doing it?". Because the mind doesn't care. What the mind cares about is comfort, and the dopamine that comes from watching TV shows gives a lot of comfort. It takes a lot of will to break a cycle driven by comfort, and it takes a little will to stay away from the cycle after you break away from it too. That's why it's so easy to fall back into the cycle, because a certain part of you wants to fall back into the cycle. That's the power comfort has on your mind.

So what should I do to stay away from this vicious cycle of sleeplessness? Simple. Stay away from comfort, whenever possible. And get used to staying uncomfortable.

Will it help you stay away from sacrificing sleep to watch movies? Yes. Will it help you to break or stay away from any habit that you don't want to have? Yes. Will you build will power? Yes, because being comfortable with staying uncomfortable is what willpower is (at least, that's what I think). Will it lead to a happy life? I have no clue. Is it even possible to get used to staying uncomfortable? I will try and let you know.

There ends my aimless but somewhat controlled chain of thought. Hope it helped!

I'm writing this for myself, to get a perspective, and for others who would like to know others' perspectives. I mix up "I"s and "you"s because it just feels good. If any of this troubles you, please stay away from it. Or get used to it, in which case you'll know that you have been inspired by my article.
