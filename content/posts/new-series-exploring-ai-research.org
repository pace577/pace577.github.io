Exploring research areas in pursuit of the ideal graduate specialization.
Have you ever been in a situation where you are presented with a choices from which you must pick one, and you are unable to make a choice because you like all of them? If you were, how do you begin to solve this problem? One approach is to pick any one and go with it, but there is a remote chance that you will not like your choice in the future and regret not thinking before making the choice.

Would you risk it anyways and pick a random choice? I know I wouldn't. I like to think that all people run on loss functions in life, and mine is regret. My zero regret policy helped me through my life so far, and while it makes me go through some troublesome experiences (sometimes I wonder if I'm masochistic) it always led me to satisfaction at the end.

I'm in a situation similar to the one described above, but my sample space of choices are all the possible graduate programs in the world. I'm an undergraduate engineering student wanting to pursue higher studies in a field that I like. The problem is that I like everything, and I'm unable to decide which program to pick! Moreover, there are so many programs that fit my interest but are probably out of my reach due to financial reasons and/or inadequacy of experience in the field.

Nevertheless, I decided to explore all the research areas that grab my attention and make a brief log of them. So why not put this log on the internet for interested people to read? Hoping that it will help those who have the same problem as me, I'll get started!

This will be a series of posts covering different research areas related to Artificial Intelligence and Robotics (and related topics). I'm not sure what would be an optimal structure for a post in this series, so I'm going to initially use the following structure and modify it in later posts if necessary.

- Every post is dedicated to a particular /Research Group/Lab/ (or research done by a particular professor). I'll introduce the professor running the lab and briefly talk about their research interests.
- I'll go through some of the papers they published (need not be the most popular ones) and add some remarks about them.
- I'll conclude by talking about how cool they are (No, I will not quantify their coolness. Please stop quantifying reputation.) and thinking out loud about what kind of people would be a good fit for that research lab.

Note that whatever I write in my posts *isn't exhaustive by any means*. This is just an accumulation of some information I acquired from the internet and other sources. I will be providing links to the website (if it exists) of the research lab I'll be writing about, which one can access to obtain more information about that lab.

See you in the next post!
